WxNotify is a service that sends notifications for all types of weather phenomena, in addition to law enforcement and governmental alerts.

F.A.Q.

Why do I need a service like WxNotify?
Great question!

The frequency and severity of phenomena such as tornadoes, hurricanes, and droughts continues to increase year after year due to climate change. Because of this, the need for dependable severe weather alerts is needed now more than ever. You will find that WxNotify offers the most alerting options of any alerting service out there.
Do I still need WxNotify if I'm already receiving alerts on my phone?
We think so!

While it is true that all cell phone users are now receiving Wireless Emergency Alerts (WEA), these alerts only offer the required information and are not customizable. WxNotify is the perfect fit for those who want full control over all of their weather, law enforcement, and governmental alert notifications.
What kinds of weather phenomena does does WxNotify send alerts for?
WxNotify receives its weather data in the form of textual "products" directly from the National Weather Service. Products includes things like watches, warnings, and forecasts and are available for everything from life-threatening events like tornadoes, severe thunderstorms, flash floods, and hurricanes, to the less serious advisories for wind, fog, heat, & cold weather. You may choose from over 150 types of products to create your alerts.
Does WxNotify have an iOS and/or Android app?
Not yet.

However, this is currently in the works! The mobile apps will feature push notifications for alerts, and show current conditions & the 7-day forecast for the user's location. Upon launch, the mobile apps will only be available for the paid subscriptions. Please stay tuned to this website for future annoucements for native app availability!
Why won't I be able to use the mobile apps with a free Tier 1 account?
Fair question.

The development and publishing of native apps to their respective stores have associated fees, and it's becoming increasingly difficult to absorb all of these and other associated expenses. So for now, we will be offering use of the mobile apps to the paid tiers to help us cover these costs.
Where did the name WxNotify come from?
Glad you asked!

"Wx" is often used throughout the weather community as an abbreviation for weather. Since the service primarily sends notifications for weather-related events, the name seemed like a natural fit.
